### 1. Why the Camera Feature Didn't Work (and How I Fixed It)

The backend endpoint (`POST /sensors/emotion`) was working perfectly, but the Flutter frontend **wasn't actually capturing or sending any images**.

I have added the necessary logic to your frontend:
1. **Added `camera: ^0.11.0`** to your `pubspec.yaml`.
2. **Created `CameraService`** (`lib/services/camera_service.dart`) which hooks into the device's front camera and starts a background timer to take a picture every 5 seconds. It converts the image to base64 and POSTs it to the `/sensors/emotion` API.
3. **Updated `SessionProvider`** to automatically initialize the camera when you click "Start Session" and safely dispose of it when you click "End Session".

**⚠️ IMPORTANT: What you need to do before running the app again:**
Because the app now uses the device camera, you must add camera permissions to your native configurations.

*   **For Android:** 
    Open `frontend/android/app/build.gradle` and ensure `minSdkVersion` is at least **21**:
    ```gradle
    defaultConfig {
        minSdkVersion 21
    }
    ```
    Open `frontend/android/app/src/main/AndroidManifest.xml` and add these right above `<application>`:
    ```xml
    <uses-permission android:name="android.permission.CAMERA" />
    <uses-feature android:name="android.hardware.camera" />
    <uses-feature android:name="android.hardware.camera.autofocus" />
    ```
*   **For iOS:**
    Open `frontend/ios/Runner/Info.plist` and add:
    ```xml
    <key>NSCameraUsageDescription</key>
    <string>We need camera access to analyze your facial emotions during the session.</string>
    ```

Once you add these and rebuild the app (using `flutter clean` then `flutter run`), your camera will silently take pictures every 5 seconds while you fill out the questionnaire and send them to the backend!

### 2. What Happens Behind the Questionnaire & How the Model Evaluates It

When you are taking a session, the backend doesn't just look at your questionnaire answers. It uses a **Multimodal Machine Learning Approach**. Here is the exact flow:

**Step 1: Data Collection (During the Session)**
*   As you answer the questionnaire, your responses are mapped to a scale (`cal_score` from 0 to 4) and saved in the database.
*   Simultaneously, the new `CameraService` is sending your face to the backend every 5 seconds. The backend runs `DeepFace` on these frames, scores your stress based on your facial expressions (e.g., Sad/Angry/Fear = higher stress weight), and stores the average distress score.
*   (If connected, EEG and BP data are also streaming in the background).

**Step 2: Feature Engineering (When you click "End Session")**
When the session ends, the backend triggers `ml/feature_builder.py`. This script pulls all the data you just generated and compresses it into a **16-element feature vector**:
*   **Questionnaire Data:** 5 features (Average score of Stage 1, Stage 2, Stage 3, Stage 4, Stage 5).
*   **Facial Emotion Data:** 1 feature (The weighted distress score from all pictures taken during the session).
*   **EEG/Brainwaves:** 4 features (Average Delta, Theta, Alpha, and Beta wave powers). *Since you don't have EEG connected right now, the backend safely handles this by assigning zeroes.*
*   **Cardiovascular (BP):** 3 features (Average Systolic, Diastolic, and Pulse rate).
*   **Academic/Work Metrics:** 3 features (For students: CGPA trend, attendance drop, failed courses. For teachers: workload, admin duties, feedback trend).

**Step 3: Machine Learning Evaluation**
This 16-number array is then fed into a **Random Forest Classifier** model (`ml/predictor.py`). 
*   The model looks at the relationships between all 16 variables based on thousands of synthetic training records. 
*   For example, it might notice: *"Their questionnaire score indicates mild stress, but their facial emotion distress score is extremely high and their CGPA trend is dropping rapidly."*
*   The model calculates probabilities for 4 different outcomes and picks the one with the highest confidence:
    1.  **Normal**
    2.  **Calm Down** (Mild stress detected)
    3.  **See Psychologist** (Moderate to high risk)
    4.  **Emergency** (Critical risk)

Finally, it returns this recommendation and the percentage confidence back to your Flutter app, which then redirects you to the detailed Result Dashboard!
